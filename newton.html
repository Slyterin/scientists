About Isaac Newton

Sir Isaac Newton PRS was born on 4 January 1643, Woolsthorpe Manor House in the UK. He was an English mathematician, physicist, astronomer, alchemist, theologian, and author. He was a key figure in the philosophical revolution known as the Enlightenment. He obtained his education from Trinity College (1667–1668), Trinity College (1661–1665), The King's School (1655–1660). Isaac Newton made the law known as “The law of gravity” which got famous in the year 1869. Isaac Newton spoke languages which are English, Latin and Greek and his nationality is British. He took his last breath at the age of 84 on 31 March 1727, Kensington, London, United Kingdom and his last words were "the great ocean of truth lay all undiscovered before me.".
